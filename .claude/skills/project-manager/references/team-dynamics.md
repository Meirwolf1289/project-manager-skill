# Team Dynamics & Health

## Tuckman's Stages of Team Development

### 1. Forming
- **Behavior:** Polite, cautious, dependent on leader
- **SM Role:** Set clear expectations, define roles, establish working agreements
- **Signs:** People ask lots of questions, avoid conflict, look for guidance

### 2. Storming
- **Behavior:** Conflicts emerge, push back on processes, frustration
- **SM Role:** Facilitate healthy conflict, mediate, normalize disagreement
- **Signs:** Debates about approach, resistance to tasks, personality clashes
- **Key:** This is necessary and healthy. Don't suppress it — channel it.

### 3. Norming
- **Behavior:** Team finds its rhythm, mutual respect, shared processes
- **SM Role:** Step back, reinforce good practices, refine working agreements
- **Signs:** People help each other, conflicts resolve quickly, consistent velocity

### 4. Performing
- **Behavior:** High autonomy, self-organizing, focused on outcomes
- **SM Role:** Remove obstacles, protect the team, coach from the side
- **Signs:** Team makes decisions independently, proactive problem-solving, high morale

### 5. Adjourning
- **Behavior:** Project ending, team dispersing
- **SM Role:** Celebrate achievements, capture lessons learned, support transitions

## Psychological Safety

Based on Amy Edmondson's research — the single strongest predictor of team performance.

### What It Looks Like
- People ask questions without fear of looking stupid
- Mistakes are discussed openly as learning opportunities
- Team members challenge ideas respectfully
- Everyone speaks roughly equal amounts in meetings

### How to Build It
1. **Model vulnerability** — leader admits mistakes first
2. **Frame work as learning** — "what did we learn?" not "who messed up?"
3. **Invite input explicitly** — "What am I missing?" "Who sees this differently?"
4. **Respond productively to bad news** — thank the messenger
5. **Separate people from problems** — critique the approach, not the person

### Warning Signs (Low Safety)
- Silence in meetings
- "I told you so" culture
- People CC managers on routine emails
- No one volunteers information about problems
- Retros have no real issues raised

## Conflict Resolution

### Thomas-Kilmann Model

| Style | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Competing** | Emergency decisions, safety issues |
| **Collaborating** | Complex issues where both sides' concerns are important |
| **Compromising** | Time-pressured, both sides have equal power |
| **Avoiding** | Trivial issues, or when emotions need to cool down |
| **Accommodating** | When you're wrong, or the issue matters more to the other person |

### Conflict Resolution Steps
1. **Acknowledge** — "I can see this is important to both of you"
2. **Separate facts from feelings** — "What happened?" vs "How did that make you feel?"
3. **Find shared goals** — "You both want the project to succeed"
4. **Explore options together** — "What would work for both of you?"
5. **Agree on next steps** — concrete actions with follow-up

## Team Health Check (Spotify Model)

Rate each dimension Green / Amber / Red:

| Dimension | Green | Amber | Red |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----|
| **Delivering Value** | We deliver great stuff regularly | We deliver but not always what matters | We rarely deliver, or it's not valuable |
| **Speed** | We deliver fast | Acceptable pace but could improve | Too slow, bottlenecks everywhere |
| **Tech Quality** | Clean code, good practices | Some tech debt, manageable | Tech debt is crippling us |
| **Learning** | We learn and grow constantly | Some learning happens | Stagnant, no growth |
| **Mission** | We know why we exist and it inspires us | We sort of know | No idea why we're doing this |
| **Fun** | Love coming to work | It's OK | Dread |
| **Teamwork** | Great collaboration, trust | Decent but siloed at times | Dysfunctional |
| **Support** | We get help when we need it | Sometimes | We're on our own |

Track quarter-over-quarter for trends. Focus improvement efforts on dimensions trending from Green to Amber.
