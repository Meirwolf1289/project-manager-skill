# Retrospective Formats

## Start / Stop / Continue

The classic format. Simple, accessible, works for any team.

**Structure:**
- **Start** — what should we begin doing?
- **Stop** — what should we stop doing?
- **Continue** — what's working well and should continue?

**Facilitation:**
1. 5 min silent brainstorming (sticky notes or digital board)
2. Group similar items
3. Dot-vote on top items (3 votes per person)
4. Discuss top 3-5 items (10 min each)
5. Convert to 2-3 action items with owners

**Best for:** New teams, general-purpose, when you need a quick retro

## 4Ls (Liked / Learned / Lacked / Longed For)

Growth-oriented format that emphasizes learning.

**Structure:**
- **Liked** — what did you enjoy?
- **Learned** — what did you learn?
- **Lacked** — what was missing?
- **Longed For** — what do you wish you had?

**Facilitation:**
1. 5 min per quadrant
2. Share and discuss patterns
3. "Lacked" and "Longed For" are your improvement areas

**Best for:** Teams focused on growth, after a training or new process adoption

---

## Sailboat

Visual metaphor that makes abstract concepts tangible.

**Structure:**
- **Wind** (sails) — what propelled us forward?
- **Anchor** — what held us back?
- **Rocks** (ahead) — what risks do we see coming?
- **Island** (destination) — what's our goal?

**Facilitation:**
1. Draw a sailboat on a whiteboard/digital canvas
2. Team adds items to each area
3. Focus discussion on anchors (biggest impediments) and rocks (upcoming risks)
4. Action items: how to cut anchors and navigate around rocks

**Best for:** Visual thinkers, when the team feels stuck, mid-project check-ins

---

## Mad / Sad / Glad

Emotion-focused format that surfaces team morale issues.

**Structure:**
- **Mad** — what frustrated you?
- **Sad** — what disappointed you?
- **Glad** — what made you happy?

**Facilitation:**
1. Emphasize this is a safe space — emotions are valid
2. 5 min silent brainstorming
3. Share without judgment
4. Look for patterns in Mad/Sad — these are your improvement areas
5. Celebrate Glad items — recognize good work

**Best for:** After a difficult sprint, when morale is low, when you suspect unspoken frustrations

---

## Starfish

Five categories for more nuanced feedback than Start/Stop/Continue.

**Structure:**
- **Keep Doing** — working well, continue as-is
- **Less Of** — reduce but don't eliminate
- **More Of** — increase, doing it but not enough
- **Stop Doing** — eliminate entirely
- **Start Doing** — new practices to try

**Facilitation:**
1. Draw a 5-pointed star on the board
2. 7 min brainstorming across all categories
3. "Less Of" and "More Of" provide nuance that binary Start/Stop misses
4. Prioritize by impact

**Best for:** Mature teams, when Start/Stop/Continue feels too coarse, process fine-tuning

---

## Facilitation Tips (All Formats)

- **Time-box ruthlessly** — retros that run long lose energy
- **Silent brainstorming first** — prevents groupthink and ensures quieter voices are heard
- **Dot voting** — democratic prioritization (3 votes per person)
- **Rotate facilitator** — shared ownership of the process
- **Track action items** — if last sprint's actions weren't done, discuss why before creating new ones
- **Vary the format** — using the same format every sprint leads to stale retros
- **End on a positive** — even after tough sprints, acknowledge what went well
